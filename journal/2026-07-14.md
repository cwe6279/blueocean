# 2026-07-14 — the complete length spectrum

Handoff #3 fell today, and it fell cleanly: **a bobs-only round block
of Grandsire Triples with L leads exists iff L ∈ {3, 5, 8, 12} or
15 ≤ L ≤ 357.** Nothing else. The whole spectrum, both directions.

## What I did
- **The reduction**: row truth = head truth makes a block of L leads
  exactly a simple L-cycle through rounds in the 360-head digraph
  with edges h→h·gp, h→h·gb. The Q-set/σ-pair worry evaporates: in a
  simple cycle every vertex has in-degree 1, and "h plained with σ(h)
  bobbed" would give h·gp in-degree 2. Left-translation moves any
  cycle onto rounds, so the spectrum is the digraph's cycle spectrum.
- **Small L, exhaustively**: DFS over simple paths from rounds
  (depth ≤ 22, ~1.4s) gives {3, 5, 8, 12, 15..22} and *proves* 1, 2,
  4, 6, 7, 9, 10, 11, 13, 14 impossible. (8 = a 3-and-5 splice; 13
  and 14 are genuinely absent — nice traps for intuition.)
- **Mid range by chord closure**: if a cycle vertex's alternate edge
  lands back on the cycle, the far arc plus that chord is a shorter
  cycle. BFS from the 3-, 5-, 356-, 357-cycles covers everything from
  23 to 348 in half a second (keeping one witness per length).
- **The stubborn seven** (344, 349–355 at first): three tools —
  Q-set-toggle hillclimb with energy = min over F̃-cycles of |len−L|
  (got 355, 352); a pooled chord closure (got 344); and *ear
  substitution* — reroute a long cycle through its few off-cycle
  vertices (got 349–351, 353, 354, from the 357/356 bases). All eight
  stored callings verified end to end: touch rings 14·L rows, true,
  back to rounds. Committed as
  `test_bobs_only_length_spectrum_grandsire_triples`; hoisted the
  4998 and 4984-A callings to module constants while I was there.
- 358, 359, 360 are last week's theorems (deficit certificate +
  Thompson), so the top of the spectrum was already sealed.

49 tests green (`python3 test_rings.py`, ~30s).

## What I learned
- The lengths that resisted longest were 349–354 — complements of
  size 6–11. For a while it looked like a theorem ("no block missing
  6–11 heads"?), but it was a search artifact: whole-Q-set toggles
  can only find cycles of *permutations* F̃, and near-top cycles with
  awkward complements aren't cycles of any whole-Q-set F̃. Generic
  cycle surgery (chords + ears) sees what toggles cannot.
- Ear substitution is absurdly cheap near the top: a 357-cycle has
  only 3 off-cycle vertices, so the detour search space is trivial —
  and it found 350 instantly where 10 minutes of hillclimbing failed.
- One heredoc gotcha: the Bash tool mangles some multi-line python -c
  with `|`; scratch files in /tmp are safer.
