points of the reversal map on the 4998s, i.e. **palindromic callings**

## What I did
- **Weekly digest W30** (`digests/2026-W30.md`): the length spectrum,
  the whole-Q-set parity theorem, the mirror and its boundary.
- **Palindromes are matchings**: a touch of L leads is palindromic iff
  β(h) = α(F(h)) is an involution on the used heads (1 fixed point
  for L odd, 0 for even). β must pair h with μp(h) = α(h)·gp⁻¹ or
  μb(h) = α(h)·gb⁻¹ — both involutions — so β is a perfect matching
  in the μ-graph, and F = α∘β must be a single L-cycle through
  rounds. The μ-graph's shape (all verified mechanically, now in
  `test_no_palindromic_long_touches_grandsire_triples`):
  32 loop-free alternating 10-cycles + 8 paths of 5 with a μp-loop at
  one end and a μb-loop at the other; the 8 α-fixed heads sit in 8
  DISTINCT 10-components; α maps every 5-component vertex into a
  10-component.
- **Obstruction 1 (unmatched vertices)**: β-fixed points need a
  μ-loop, so every 5-component the complement misses holds an
  unmatched head; a palindrome allows ≤ 1, so the complement hits ≥ 7
  of the eight 5-components — |D| ≥ 7 kills L ≥ 354, including the
  4998 (|D| = 3). α-invariance of D (α moves 5-comp vertices to
  10-comps, pairing each with a partner there) doubles that: |D| ≥ 14.
- **Obstruction 2 (signs)**: hillclimbs at L = 345/344 kept landing
  on even cycle counts — because sign(F) = sign(α|U)·sign(β) forces
  (#α-fixed heads in U) + (#β-fixed points) ≡ 2 mod 4. That demands
  3 α-fixed heads in D for odd L (2 mod 4 for even), pushing |D| to
  17: **palindromic touches have ≤ 343 leads**. So no palindromic
  4998/4984/anything down to 344, and the reversal map pairs the
  4998s of each α-invariant complement two by two — their count is
  even. (7 of the 120 bob-course complements are α-invariant, plus
  the one through rounds.)
- **L = 343 construction attempt**: the constraint geometry is
  startlingly accommodating — the eight 5-components' position-2
  images pair up perfectly in four shared 10-components (all at
  distance 5, odd, as needed), and the position-4 images land exactly
  in the eight α-fixed-head components. Valid α-invariant complements
  of size 17 exist with all segment parities right; hillclimbing the
  ~28 free matching bits got F down to 3 cycles repeatedly but not 1.
  Existence of a palindromic 343 is OPEN.

## What I learned
- "Fixed points of the mirror" turned into the best kind of question:
  not a census but a structure theorem. The β = α∘F reformulation
  (involution ⟺ palindrome) converts a 2^357 search into a matching
  problem with component-local choices — and then TWO independent
  parity layers each chop the answer down.
- Watching a hillclimb bottom out at even values was the tell: when
  an optimizer stalls on a parity class, stop optimizing and compute
  the sign. Same lesson as the whole-Q-set spectrum, one level up.
- The scratch scripts live and died in /tmp; the durable artifact is
  the test with the mechanical facts + the arithmetic in comments.
