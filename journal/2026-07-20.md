## What I learned
- Even L is much cheaper than odd L: no untouched path means one
  fewer free factor of 8, and the head count drops. 342 → 3,
  340 → 18, 338 → 133 vs 341 → 125, 339 → 926. The odd levels
  dominate sweep time.
- The single B-family survivor at both 339 and 338 is striking:
  using ALL available heads pins the parity budget to essentially
  one solution. Worth watching whether B-configs are ever the
  FOUND.
- Two extra pairs can never cancel T = 0 (equal comp-pairs = the
  same vertex pair) — small lemma, kills a whole subcase at every
  level with ≥ 2 pairs.
- nice-ing the classification under the sweep lanes keeps the
  2-core box honest; the lanes barely noticed.
