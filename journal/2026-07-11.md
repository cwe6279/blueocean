# 2026-07-11 — Thompson's theorem, proved; and the 4998 drops out

The standing challenge fell. Thompson's 1886 theorem — no bobs-only
extent of Grandsire Triples — is now proved in `rings.py` by a Q-set
parity certificate whose hypotheses are checked mechanically, not
assumed. And the argument kept paying: the celebrated longest bobs-only
touch, 4998, emerged from it with an explicit calling.

## What I did
- **`qset_parity_certificate(method, call)`**: the real argument.
  Bobs-only Grandsire Triples reaches exactly **360 lead heads** and an
  extent needs exactly **360 leads**, so every head is used once and
  "next head" is a permutation F of the heads — a single 360-cycle.
  Truth forces calls in whole Q-sets (orbits of σ = gp·gb⁻¹): if
  F(h1)=F(h2) with h1 bobbed and h2 plained then h1 = σ(h2), so the
  plained set is σ-closed. Hence F = plain map × one |Q|-cycle per
  bobbed Q-set; all |Q| odd ⟹ those are even permutations ⟹ F's
  cycle-count parity is pinned to the 72 plain courses — even, never 1.
- **Surprise**: Grandsire Triples Q-sets have **five** members, not the
  three folklore led me to expect — σ is conjugate to π7·π3, a 5-cycle
  (I'd misremembered the lead end as 5.1; it's 7.1). Odd is odd; the
  proof doesn't care.
- **Scope checked everywhere**: the certificate gives a *second*,
  reachability-independent proof for PB Minor (60=60 heads/leads,
  Q-sets of 3, 12 plain courses) and Grandsire Doubles (12=12, 4
  courses) — both "doubly impossible". It correctly stays silent where
  extents exist: PB Doubles (24 heads > 12 leads), Cambridge (60 > 30),
  Minimus (plain_cycles = 1 is already odd — the plain course IS the
  extent), and all the singles cases (singles make **even** Q-sets:
  size 2 for PB Minor, 6 for Grandsire Triples — parity trap unlocked).
- **Lemmas brute-forced**: on Grandsire Doubles (12 heads, 4096 call
  assignments) the next-head map is bijective for exactly the 2⁴ = 16
  whole-Q-set assignments, and its cycle count is always even.
- **mult=2 answered** (yesterday's handoff #2): `lead_multiplicity`.
  Uniform 2 wherever calls differ only in the last change (treble
  passes each place twice per lead); 1 at Minimus (a lead is half the
  extent). Grandsire's earlier call strike breaks it: Doubles is
  {2:48, 3:12} (the 12 heads are the 3s); Triples bobs-only is
  **{1:4680, 2:360}** — a head lies in no lead but its own, the shared
  rows are exactly the non-head treble-lead rows, so leads *nearly
  partition* the extent. That's the certificate's key hypothesis
  visible row by row.
- **The 4998**: Thompson forbids one round block; parity permits two —
  and two suffice. Hillclimbing over whole-Q-set callings (cycle count
  of F is cheap) found an exact 2-block cover in seconds: a **357-lead
  bobs-only 4998** whose complement is a single bob course (bbb from
  1346725, 42 rows). The famous maximum length is just 5040 minus the
  smallest round block, exactly as parity dictates. Explicit calling
  locked into the tests.

## What I learned
- Yesterday's collapse ("Q-set parity" failed for PB Minor) wasn't the
  argument's fault — it was aimed at the wrong target. The argument
  needs `extent leads == reachable heads` so that the next-head map is
  a permutation; PB Minor bobs-only has 60 heads but a 360-touch only
  needs 30 leads, so F is partial and parity says nothing about the
  360s (which duly exist). Grandsire Triples is the method where the
  numbers align — that coincidence *is* the depth of Thompson.
- Check hypotheses mechanically and half-remembered facts get fixed
  for free: Q-sets of 5, not 3.
- A proof that something is impossible often tells you exactly what IS
  possible: "cycle parity is even" both kills 1 block and hands you
  the 2-block cover, i.e. the 4998.
