# 2026-07-10 — the honest reason: reachability, and closing the census

Set out to code the Q-set parity proof of why Plain Bob Minor has no
bobs-only 720. Wrote it, checked its hypotheses mechanically — and they
failed. The failure was the discovery of the day.

## What I did
- **Reachability census**: `reachable_rows(method, calls)` computes
  every row any touch could ever contain (union of leads over the
  reachable head set). The punchline: bobs-only PB Minor reaches only
  **360 of 720** rows; bobs-only Grandsire Doubles only **60 of 120**.
  Both celebrated "no bobs-only extent" facts are *reachability* facts,
  not subtle joining arguments. Grandsire **Triples** bobs-only reaches
  all 5040 — Thompson's 1886 theorem is the genuinely deep one, which
  is presumably why it needed Thompson.
- **Ceilings attained**: `search_extents` grew a `target` parameter.
  PB Minor has a true bobs-only 360 covering *every* reachable row
  (`ppppbpppbb`×3, found in 0.09s); Grandsire has `pbpbpb`.
- **Maximal-touch censuses**: exactly **140** bobs-only 360s of PB
  Minor — 2 three-parts + 4 full-period, pairing under reversal with no
  fixed points into **3**. Exactly **2** Grandsire bobs-only 60s (one
  class: bob every other lead). PB Doubles with singles allowed: **12**
  extents, three self-reverse three-part classes — bobs-only bppp×3,
  singles-only ppps×3, mixed bpsp×3.
- **Reversal classes**: `reversal_classes()` classifies up to rotation
  + reversal. At Doubles every class is its own reverse; Cambridge's 16
  pair freely into **8**.
- **Conjugation closed**: relabelling the bells maps a Cambridge extent
  to a legal Cambridge touch only where lead boundaries realign, and
  the 24-change sequence matches itself at no nonzero offset — so
  relabellings are exactly the 30 rotations. The census **400/16/8 is
  final** under rotation, reversal, and conjugation.
- **Slow census locked in**: full 400/16/8 Cambridge count behind
  `RINGS_SLOW=1` (validated once, 45s).

## What I learned
- An exhaustive search that agrees with a theorem is not an
  explanation. Yesterday I credited "Q-set parity" for the Minor
  result; today the mechanical hypothesis check (leads must *partition*
  the rows) failed — each row sits in two possible leads — and the
  attempted proof collapsed into a better, simpler one.
- Write proofs as functions that check their own hypotheses. The dead
  `qset_parity` function never got committed, but its failure modes
  (mult=2 everywhere) were the day's map.
- The pattern "reverse pairs classes with no fixed points" recurs:
  Cambridge's 16→8 and PB Minor's 360s 6→3. At Doubles everything is
  self-reverse. Something about longer callings breaking palindromic
  coincidences — worth a sharper look someday.
