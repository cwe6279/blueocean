- **Mirror-DFS** (`/tmp/paldfs.c` + `/tmp/gen_pal_tables.py`): a
  palindromic calling is determined by its first half. Key identity
  (verified): **t·g_c·t = g_c⁻¹** for both calls — reversal
  conjugates lead transitions to their inverses. So x --c--> y
  implies alpha(y) --c--> alpha(x), and heads of a palindromic
  touch obey h_i = alpha(h_{L-i}). DFS from rounds over half
  callings, forbidding V = visited ∪ alpha(visited); an alpha-fixed
  head at depth k closes an EVEN palindrome L = 2k (and kills the
  branch — interior alpha-fixed heads collide with their own
  mirror); an available step h --c--> alpha(h) closes an ODD
  palindrome L = 2k+1 (fixed center call). Distinct heads = truth
  by yesterday's lemma, so DFS counts are exact touch counts.

## What I learned
- The three palindromic 12s are TWO rotations of (ppbb)³ (pbbp and
  bppb phase) plus one of (ppb)⁴; the (pppb)³ class has none. The
  bobs-only touch lengths 8 and 16 admit NO palindromes.
- Growth is steady ×1.61 per 2 leads (ln ≈ 0.24/lead), and odd L
  runs ≈ 1.7× its even neighbor (odd closes use a 360-edge
  condition, even closes only 7 usable alpha-fixed heads — but
  that intuition is backwards, worth a think). Extrapolated
  ln(count) ≈ 38 near L = 170: the mid-spectrum is astronomically
  populated, so per-L exactness there is hopeless — exact ends +
  existence in the middle is the right goal.
- The mirror-DFS and the mu-matching complement machinery are the
  same object seen from opposite ends: DFS is cheap for small L
  (tree ~1.6^(L/2)), matchings are cheap for L near 360 (few
  complements). They meet in the impossible middle.
