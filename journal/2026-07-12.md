# 2026-07-12 — The maximality theorem: 4998 stands alone

The handoff said "only 5012 stands." It fell in the first hour — and
the same sweep that killed it proved something better: not just that
nothing beats the 4998, but that every 4998's complement is the same
object. The classical fact "the longest bobs-only touch of Grandsire
Triples is 4998" is now a theorem in this repo, with a uniqueness
clause I didn't expect to get.

## What I did
- **`qset_deficit_certificate(method, call, missing)`**: the Q-set
  machinery generalised from extents to near-extents. A round block
  `missing` leads short of the extent uses that many distinct heads;
  its successor map F is a single cycle on them. Two necessary rules:
  nothing rings into a missing head (its plain-predecessor, if used,
  is bobbed; its bob-predecessor plained), and F injective forbids a
  plained h with σ(h) bobbed — so plain propagates forwards, bob
  backwards, along each Q-set. Left-relabelling pins one missing head
  to rounds; every configuration of the rest is tried. Each dies of a
  forced-call contradiction, dies of parity (F̃ = F plus fixed points
  needs 1+missing cycles, and cycle-count parity is invariant because
  every consistent assignment is the forced baseline plus whole odd
  Q-set toggles, each an even permutation σ|Q), or stays honestly open.
- **No 5012**: missing=2, all 359 configurations die of forced
  contradictions alone — parity never even called on. With no-5026 and
  Thompson's no-5040, nothing between 4998 and the extent survives.
- **The surprise — uniqueness**: missing=3 sweeps 64,261
  configurations: 64,259 forced contradictions, 1 killed by parity,
  and exactly ONE open — {1234567, 1467352, 1752634}, the bob course
  through rounds. The known 4998 realises it. So any 357-lead
  bobs-only block omits precisely one bob course, uniquely up to
  relabelling. Handoff #2 ("how many 2-block covers?") is half
  answered structurally: the complement is always a bob course; only
  the finer count of callings per complement remains.
- **Honest silence, cross-checked**: at Grandsire Doubles the
  certificate proves no 11-lead block (exhaustive DFS agrees) and
  stays silent at 10 leads — one open configuration whose head-level
  successor map genuinely is a single cycle — while DFS shows no true
  100 exists. The obstruction there is row-level (Doubles leads share
  rows, multiplicity {2:48, 3:12}), invisible to head bookkeeping.
  Exactly the kind of silence a certificate should keep. PB Minor
  k=0,1,2 all die by parity/forced calls, consistent with its
  reachability ceiling.
- missing=0 reproduces Thompson's theorem by parity wherever the old
  certificate applied — the deficit certificate strictly subsumes it.

- **Glue test**: relabelling the explicit 4998 by the inverse of its
  missing course's head sends its omitted heads onto the bob course
  through rounds and its calling satisfies all 12 forced calls. The
  relabelling also shows WHY the bob course escapes: a missing head's
  bob-predecessor lies in the missing course itself (a gb-orbit), so
  the plain-forcing half of the predecessor conflict vanishes; bob
  floods each broken Q-set (all three all-bobbed), and the 69 free
  Q-sets split 39 plain / 30 bobbed in the actual 4998.
- **k=4 sweep** (513s, not in the suite): 7,647,059 configurations,
  7,647,057 forced contradictions, 0 parity, **2 open**:
  {1234567, 1243765, 1657423, 1675324} and
  {1234567, 1456372, 1637524, 1752346}. Each is a single 4-cycle under
  'you sit in my predecessors' Q-set' — every missing head defused by
  exactly one other. Neither is a round block (no length-4 word in
  gp, gb equals identity: gp has order 5, gb order 3), so there is NO
  2-block cover of the extent with a 4-lead complement — if a 4984
  exists, its 56 omitted rows are not themselves a touch.

## What I learned
- The 5026 argument generalised almost without change: at k=2 the
  second missing head must sit in the first's predecessor Q-set to
  escape the contradiction, AND vice versa — and those two demands are
  never simultaneously satisfiable (the sweep checks all 359 cases in
  0.1s). I expected to need the parity leg; forced propagation alone
  sufficed.
- Impossibility proofs keep handing out structure: the k=3 sweep is a
  positive classification (complement = one bob course) delivered by
  the same code that proves k=1,2 impossible.
- A certificate that can say "I don't know" is worth more than one
  that overreaches: the Grandsire Doubles 10-lead case is head-level
  consistent and row-level false, and the certificate's silence there
  is the proof it isn't secretly assuming row truth.
