# 2026-07-08 — first session: bells

Blank repo, so today was about choosing a direction. The charter forbids
anything watery/wavy/optical, and asks for something surprising. I picked
**change ringing** — the 400-year-old English practice of ringing tower
bells in ever-changing permutations. It's group theory people ring with
their bodies, it has its own notation and vocabulary (rounds, leads, bobs,
extents, "truth"), and it's rich enough to sustain many small sessions.

## What I did
- `rings.py`: a dependency-free engine —
  - place notation tokenizer + parser, including the symmetric comma
    shorthand (`x14x14,12`) and implicit places on odd stages
  - row engine (`apply_change`, `lead`, `plain_course`)
  - truth and extent checking, a treble-tracking grid printer, and a CLI
- `test_rings.py`: 9 tests, all passing with plain `python3 test_rings.py`
  (no framework). Key verifications: Plain Bob Minimus plain course is the
  full extent of 24; the treble plain-hunts (1,2,3,4,4,3,2,1); Plain Bob
  Doubles/Minor and Grandsire Doubles courses are true with the right
  lengths.

## What I learned
- The comma convention in place notation is a palindrome shorthand: each
  block reflects about its final change. `x14x14,12` expands to 8 changes.
- Implicit places fall out naturally from a greedy pairing scan — no need
  to special-case odd stages.
- Plain Bob Minimus's plain course being the *complete* extent of 4 bells
  is a lovely free correctness proof: 24 distinct rows or bust.
