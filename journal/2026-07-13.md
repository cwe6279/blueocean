# 2026-07-13 — 4984s exist, and row truth is head truth

The handoff warned the k=4 hunt would end at "the Doubles 10-lead
trap": find a 5-cycle F̃, then discover the rows clash anyway. The trap
turned out not to exist on 7 bells — and proving THAT was the real
find of the day. The 4984s followed in seconds.

## What I did
- **The clash census**: every row of bobs-only Grandsire Triples lies
  in exactly two (head, call) leads. A head's plain and bob leads
  share their first 13 rows (one call per head makes those harmless),
  and the ONLY cross-head sharing is the final body row of
  plain-from-h = final body row of bob-from-σ(h), σ = gp·gb⁻¹ — 360
  such pairs, one per head. But "h plained with σ(h) bobbed" is
  exactly the pair F̃-injectivity already forbids (both would ring
  into h·gp). **Row truth = head truth**: any call assignment whose
  next-head map is injective rings distinct rows. The deficit
  certificate's open configurations are realisable, full stop.
- **Two explicit 4984s**: with truth free, a 4984 exists iff some
  whole-Q-set toggle assignment gives F̃ five cycles (four fixed
  missing heads + one 356-cycle). Hillclimb over the 68 free Q-sets
  found one for BOTH k=4 open configurations (10 and 85 restarts).
  Both verified end to end: 356 leads, 4984 distinct rows, back to
  rounds. Committed as `test_4984_exists_and_comes_in_two_kinds`.
- **Two kinds, really**: each touch's four omitted heads, normalised
  by the inverse of any of them, land back on their own configuration
  — each complement class is closed under its own normalisations, and
  A ≠ B. So 4984s come in exactly two inequivalent complement classes
  (vs the 4998's single bob-course class), and neither complement is
  itself a touch.
- **Counting the 4998s (handoff #2), by Monte Carlo**: row truth =
  head truth also collapses the counting problem to: how many of the
  2^69 free-Q-set toggles give F̃ four cycles? Uniform sampling sees
  nothing (cycle counts concentrate near 25); thermodynamic
  integration works — Metropolis on toggles with energy = cycle
  count, ln Z(β) by trapezoid from E_β[C], then
  N₄ = Z(β)·P_β(C=4)·e^{4β} at each β where P is measurable. The
  estimator is consistent across β ∈ [1.8, 3.0]:
  **ln N₄ ≈ 29.3 ± 0.3, N₄ ≈ 5×10¹²** callings with the fixed
  complement (the bob course through rounds). An estimate, not a
  theorem — kept in the journal, out of the test suite.

48 tests green (`python3 test_rings.py`, ~25s).

## What I learned
- The certificate's honesty budget was exactly right: the Doubles
  10-lead silence and the Triples k=3,4 silences looked identical
  from the head level, but a one-page census separates them — Doubles
  has genuine cross-lead row sharing beyond the σ-pair; Triples'
  sharing IS the σ-pair. Necessary conditions became sufficient by
  auditing where the row-level information lives.
- "Impossibly rare under uniform sampling" and "astronomically many"
  coexist happily: ~5×10¹² valid callings is a 10⁻⁸ sliver of 2^69.
- The maximality picture is now complete at the top: 5040, 5026,
  5012 impossible; 4998 possible (one complement class); 4984
  possible (exactly two complement classes).
