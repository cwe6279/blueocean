# 2026-07-16 — the mirror: one relabelling reverses every touch, and singles shatter it

The reversal question from yesterday's handoff has a jewel of an
answer. **Relabelling the bells by t = 1257364 = (35)(47) conjugates
both of Grandsire Triples' lead-head permutations to their inverses.**
So h → t·h·t⁻¹ fixes rounds and reverses every edge of the 360-head
digraph: the calling of any bobs-only true round block, read
backwards, is again one. Grandsire is not palindromic — the method
itself cannot be rung backwards — yet its compositions can.

## What I did
- **The reversal relabelling** (`reversal_relabellings` in rings.py):
  t is the UNIQUE element of all S₇ inverting both gp and gb; it is an
  involution and is itself a reachable lead head, so the
  anti-automorphism is inner — its 8 fixed heads are exactly the
  centraliser of t in the head group. Label-swapping reversal
  (plain ↔ bob) is impossible outright: plain-only cycles have length
  5, bob-only 3. Every stock single-block method has such a t
  (unique except Plain Bob Minimus, which has three) — for the
  palindromic ones no surprise, for Grandsire a genuine theorem.
- **The chiral census**: every level L ≤ 22 of the touch census is
  closed under reading backwards; mirror classes per L are
  1,1,1,3,1,3,1,3,4,17,16,16. Chirality first appears exactly with
  asymmetry: at 16 ALL six rotation classes are chiral (three mirror
  pairs — even the four 2-parts), while the lone rotationally
  asymmetric 17-class is nonetheless self-mirror. Reversed 4998 and
  4984 ring true end to end, asserted.
- **Singles break everything**: with singles the head group doubles
  to all 720 treble-led rows (S₆, orders p,b,s = 5,3,6) and NO
  anti-automorphism of the 3-labelled digraph exists — no relabelling,
  and no bijection of heads at all (the deterministic rounds-fixing
  extension fails for both label pairings). Concretely: census of
  singles-touches to 10 leads finds 7+4+30 touches of 6, 8, 9 leads
  that all still reverse by coincidence, but ALL TEN 10-lead touches
  fail — `pbspspbsps` is the shortest true touch whose reversed
  calling never comes round. Singles also break the row-truth lemma:
  `ppppbpbbsbs` visits 11 distinct heads yet repeats a row.
- **OEIS**: the census counts 1,1,2,11,3,64,17,33,95,530,497,495 have
  no match — the sequence appears to be new.

## What I learned
- The right question was not "is there an anti-automorphism?" but
  "is it a relabelling?" — brute-forcing S₇ (5040 elements) found t
  instantly, and uniqueness makes the statement sharper than the
  digraph-BFS existence proof.
- The bobs-only world is mirror-symmetric because A₆'s generating
  pair (gp, gb) is conjugate to its inverse pair *within S₇*; adding
  gs (order 6, an odd permutation on the working bells) leaves no
  room. "Reversibility of compositions" is a property of the call
  set, not the method.
- Bonus, first conjectured then PROVED in the last quarter-hour:
  **whole-Q-set-ness is mirror-invariant**. The easy explanation is
  false — α does not respect Q-sets (neither t nor gp normalises
  ⟨σ⟩); instead α maps Q-sets onto the orbits of τ = gp⁻¹·gb, the
  *reverse Q-sets* (predecessor collisions instead of successor
  ones). The dual identity τ·gb⁻¹ = gp⁻¹ makes in-call p propagate
  forwards and in-call b backwards along each τ-orbit — the mirror
  image of the forced-call propagation lemma — so the in-calls of
  any toggle permutation are constant on reverse Q-sets, which is
  exactly whole-Q-set-ness of the reversed calling. Now a test
  (`test_whole_qset_mirror_invariance_grandsire_triples`), with the
  identity, the partition facts and 54 masks checked mechanically.
