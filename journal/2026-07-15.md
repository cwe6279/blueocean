# 2026-07-15 — the whole-Q-set spectrum, and a census of short touches

Both halves of handoff #2 fell today. The star result: **the
whole-Q-set spectrum is the full length spectrum minus exactly
{350, 353, 354, 356}** — yesterday's "stubborn lengths" weren't a
search artifact after all; three of them are genuinely impossible for
toggle search, and now I know precisely why.

## What I did
- **The parity theorem**: toggling a Q-set composes the next-head
  permutation F̃ with a 5-cycle on that Q-set (the identity
  B = P∘σ⁻¹, verified mechanically), an even permutation — so every
  whole-Q-set F̃ has the sign of the plain map, and on 360 points its
  cycle count is stuck at even parity, like the 72 plain courses. A
  whole-Q-set touch of L leads puts rounds on an L-cycle, so the
  complement is an ODD number of F̃-cycles, each a digraph cycle with
  length in the known spectrum. Complement sizes 0, 1, 2, 4, 6, 7, 10
  are arithmetic impossibilities → no whole-Q-set 360 (a two-line
  Thompson for this subclass), 359, 358, 356, 354, 353, or 350.
- **Sufficiency, both ends**: hillclimbed 72-bit toggle vectors for
  L ∈ {8, 12, 17, 344–349, 351, 352, 355}; 3 is all-bob, 5 all-plain,
  and 357 is the 4998 itself — whose calling turns out to be
  whole-Q-set (never splits a Q-set between plain and bob), a nice
  standalone fact now asserted.
- **The complete spectrum**: a deterministic toggle-BFS from the 14
  stored vectors covers every remaining length 15..343 in two
  generations (~36 000 F̃s, ~6 s), each visited F̃ checked against the
  theorem in passing — the forbidden lengths never appear. So the
  whole-Q-set spectrum is exact and total, not just near the ends.
- **Complement arithmetic is sharp**: the witnesses realise exactly
  the partitions parity permits — 351 = 360−(3+3+3),
  349 = 360−(3+3+5), 347 = 360−(3+5+5), 346 = 360−(3+3+8),
  344 = 360−(3+5+8) — asserted per witness.
- **Census of short touches** (exhaustive DFS to depth 22, ~1.5 s):
  counts per L and rotation classes. Every touch of ≤ 15 leads is a
  k-part with k ≥ 3: bbb and ppppp unique; 8 = (pb)⁴ uniquely (so
  gp·gb has order 4); 12 = (pppb)³, (ppb)⁴ or (ppbb)³ only;
  15 = (pbb)⁵ uniquely. The first rotationally asymmetric touch has
  16 leads; at prime 17 all 17 callings are one full-period class.
  Counts: 1, 1, 2, 11, 3, 64, 17, 33, 95, 530, 497, 495 for
  L = 3, 5, 8, 12, 15..22.

52 tests green, suite ~31 s (`python3 test_rings.py`).

## What I learned
- Yesterday I wrote that 349–354 resisting toggles "was a search
  artifact". Half true: 349 and 351 were reachable all along (my
  hillclimb just missed them), but 350, 353, 354 — and 356 — are
  theorems. The energy landscape wasn't rough; the targets weren't
  there.
- Toggle-BFS beats random walk for coverage: a 200k-step random walk
  left 10 lengths uncovered; two BFS generations from good seeds left
  none. Seeds matter more than steps.
- 72-bit toggle vectors are a far better storage format for
  whole-Q-set touches than 350-char callings — the test reconstructs
  the calling from the vector and rings it end to end.
